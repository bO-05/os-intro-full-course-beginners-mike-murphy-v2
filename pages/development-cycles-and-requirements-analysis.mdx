# Development Cycles and Requirements Analysis

In software engineering, the development process is structured into different phases, known as **development cycles**. These cycles provide a systematic approach to building software, ensuring that the final product meets the required specifications and is delivered on time. Alongside development cycles, **requirements analysis** plays a crucial role in capturing and documenting the needs and expectations of stakeholders.

<Callout type="info">
  Understanding development cycles and requirements analysis is essential for any software engineer or project manager involved in the software development process.
</Callout>

Development cycles, such as the [Waterfall Model](/development-cycles-and-requirements-analysis/development-cycles/waterfall-model) and [Agile Model](/development-cycles-and-requirements-analysis/development-cycles/agile-model), provide a framework for organizing and managing the various stages of software development. Each cycle has its own characteristics, advantages, and limitations, making it important to choose the right approach based on the project's specific needs.

Requirements analysis, on the other hand, focuses on gathering, documenting, and managing the requirements for a software system. It involves identifying and prioritizing both [functional](/development-cycles-and-requirements-analysis/requirements-analysis/functional-requirements) and [non-functional requirements](/development-cycles-and-requirements-analysis/requirements-analysis/non-functional-requirements), ensuring that the software being developed aligns with the needs and expectations of stakeholders.

By combining effective development cycles with thorough requirements analysis, software development teams can:

- Ensure that the software meets the desired functionality and quality standards
- Reduce the risk of scope creep and project delays
- Improve communication and collaboration among team members and stakeholders
- Facilitate better decision-making throughout the development process
